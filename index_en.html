Obesity Care

Obesity is not just a weight issue

Obesity is a complex health crisis that threatens overall life, including sleep disorders, cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and mental health issues. Fundamental change begins only when both core factors—sleep and weight—are managed together.

38%
Domestic Adult Obesity Rate
As of 2023, steadily increasing
2x

5x

Estimated annual domestic cost (KRW)
Physical Disease

Cardiovascular and Metabolic Diseases

It is a key risk factor for chronic metabolic diseases such as hypertension, dyslipidemia, type 2 diabetes, and fatty liver.

Sleep Disorders

Sleep Apnea and Insomnia

Increased body fat narrows the airway, worsening sleep apnea, and poor sleep quality exacerbates obesity, creating a vicious cycle.

Musculoskeletal System

Joint and Spine Diseases

Excessive weight places constant strain on the knees, hips, and spine, causing degenerative arthritis and chronic pain.

Mental Health

Depression, Anxiety, and Low Self-Esteem

Social prejudice about body shape and hormonal imbalances increase the risk of depression and anxiety disorders, lowering the quality of life.

Respiratory System

Decreased Lung Function

Abdominal fat compresses the diaphragm, reducing lung capacity and increasing shortness of breath and fatigue during exercise.

Socio-economic

Decreased Productivity and Increased Medical Costs

Chronic fatigue and frequent illnesses lower work ability and lead to huge medical costs in the long run.

Why Sleep Improvement and Weight Management are Needed

  1. Lack of Sleep Causes Obesity A lack of sleep decreases appetite-suppressing hormones and increases appetite-stimulating hormones, leading to overeating. Basal metabolic rate also drops, so without solving sleep problems, the effect of weight loss through diet control is halved.
  2. Obesity Ruins Sleep Again As body fat accumulates, the airway narrows, worsening sleep apnea. Inability to sleep deeply repeats fatigue, decreased activity, and overeating. Obesity and sleep disorders exacerbate each other in a vicious cycle.
  3. Data and Consistency are Key to Weight Loss Reckless diets cause repeated muscle loss and yo-yo effects. Healthy weight loss and long-term maintenance are possible when diet, exercise, and sleep are managed integrally based on individual body data.

Fix your sleep, your weight will change. Change your weight, your life will change

Design a healthier daily life with data-driven obesity care

Lonely Death Prevention

Living Alone Should Not Be a Risk

With the rapid increase of single-person households and the elderly population, lonely death is no longer a special case. A system that detects silent crises in daily life in advance and provides connection and care is needed now.

Social Isolation

Deepening Disconnection and Lack of Ties

When relationships with family and neighbors are cut off, the network to request or receive help in emergencies disappears.

Late Discovery

Long-term neglect after death

Cases of not being discovered for days to weeks are frequent, leading to damaged dignity and social shock.

Health Gap

Neglect of chronic diseases, inability to respond to emergencies

Without regular health monitoring, golden time is missed in emergencies such as falls or cardiac arrest.

Mental Health

Depression, Isolation, Suicide Risk

Chronic loneliness accelerates depression and cognitive decline, sometimes leading to suicide risk among the elderly.

Absence of Silver Care

Threat to daily safety of the elderly

Even if accidents occur in high-risk fall areas like bathrooms or restrooms, there is no means for immediate detection and response.

Socio-economic

Increased Social Costs

Post-mortem processing, public welfare, mental health treatment—unprevented lonely deaths result in economic loss for society as a whole.

Why Lonely Death Prevention and Silver Care Services are Needed

  1. Abnormal signals in daily life must be detected first Detecting small signs like changes in lifestyle patterns, decreased movement, or not using the restroom in real-time with IoT sensors can prevent crises.
  2. In falls and emergencies, golden time is everything A fall in the bathroom can be fatal if not discovered alone. Smart devices close to daily life like Station and Toilet seats detect anomalies and automatically connect to guardians and emergency services.
  3. Connection and Care are the Core of Prevention 기술적 감지와 함께 정기 안부 확인, Community 연계, 심리 지원을 통해 고립된 1인 가구가 사회 안에서 안전하게 생활할 수 있도록 지속 지원합니다.

Safe even if alone, dignified even if old

Creating a society without lonely deaths through daily smart care
